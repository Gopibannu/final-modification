What is the Internet of Things (IoT)?

The Internet of Things (IoT) refers to the network of physical devices, vehicles, home appliances, and other objects that are embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity to exchange data over the internet.

These connected devices collect and share data, enabling smarter decision-making and automation across various sectors, such as healthcare, transportation, agriculture, and more.

How to Learn IoT?

Learning IoT involves understanding the concepts of connectivity, sensors, embedded systems, and data communication. Here's how you can start:

  • Start with the Basics: Understand basic electronics and microcontroller programming (e.g., Arduino, Raspberry Pi).
  • Learn about Networking: Study how devices communicate over networks, including protocols like MQTT and HTTP.
  • Work on Projects: Practical projects like home automation, weather stations, or IoT-enabled sensors will help reinforce your skills.
  • Explore Cloud Computing: Learn how IoT devices interact with cloud platforms to store and analyze data.
